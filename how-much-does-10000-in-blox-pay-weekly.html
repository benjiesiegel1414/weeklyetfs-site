The Catch: Why a 35% Yield Isn't Free Money

A distribution rate that high comes from an options income strategy layered on crypto exposure, and that combination has real trade-offs every $BLOX holder should understand before buying a single share.

Total return can lag the yield badly. Over the past year $BLOX posted a negative total return even with all its weekly distributions included. That's the crypto income trade in one sentence: the fund kept paying every week while the share price fell more than the income covered. The yield chart looks great in isolation while the position as a whole loses money.

Distributions vary. The weekly payout is not fixed. It rises and falls with options premiums, which depend heavily on crypto market volatility. Some weeks pay far more than the average, some far less, and portions can be return of capital.

Crypto concentration. The fund's holdings are crypto industry stocks plus bitcoin and ether exposure. When crypto draws down, both the share price and the options income environment move against you at the same time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does $BLOX really pay every week?
Yes, $BLOX distributes on a weekly schedule, roughly 52 payments per year.

Is the weekly payment always the same?
No. Distributions vary based on the options premiums the fund collects, so weekly amounts fluctuate with crypto volatility.

What happens to my $10,000 principal?
It rises and falls with the fund's share price. Over the past year the fund's total return was negative even including distributions, so the income stream alone doesn't tell you whether the position made money.

Does $BLOX hold bitcoin directly?
No. The fund holds crypto industry stocks and gets bitcoin and ether exposure through underlying funds, with an options overlay generating the income. It does not invest directly in cryptocurrencies.
