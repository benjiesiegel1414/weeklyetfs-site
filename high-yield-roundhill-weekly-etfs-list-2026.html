How Do Roundhill Weekly ETFs Generate Such High Yields?

Roundhill's high-yield weekly ETFs primarily use a covered call or options income strategy. Here's how it works in plain terms:

  • The fund holds assets (stocks, index exposure, or crypto) as collateral.
  • The fund sells options contracts (typically calls or put spreads) against those assets.
  • The premium collected from selling those options is distributed to investors as weekly income.
  • The result is a very high dividend yield — often 30%, 50%, 80%, or even higher on an annualized basis.

The tradeoff? These strategies can limit upside during bull markets and may result in price decay over time. That's why we track both Price Decay and Total Return on every ETF listed here.

Understanding Price Decay in Roundhill ETFs

One of the most important metrics on this page is Price Decay. Because Roundhill ETFs use options strategies to generate income, they may gradually lose share price over time — especially in volatile or down markets. This is sometimes called "NAV erosion."

However, price decay alone doesn't tell the whole story. That's why we also show Total Return — which combines price change AND all dividends paid since inception. An ETF with significant price decay can still have a positive total return if the dividends more than compensated for the price loss.

Are Roundhill Weekly ETFs Right for You?

Roundhill weekly ETFs may appeal to investors who:

  • Want consistent weekly cash flow rather than capital appreciation
  • Are in or near retirement and need regular income distributions
  • Understand that high yield = higher risk tradeoffs
  • Use these ETFs as part of a diversified income portfolio — not as a sole holding

They are generally not ideal for investors with a long time horizon seeking maximum capital growth, or those who don't understand how options-based income strategies work.
